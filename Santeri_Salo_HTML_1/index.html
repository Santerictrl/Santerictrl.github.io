Synopsis:

Interstellar is a 2014 epic science fiction film set in a dystopian future where Earth is becoming uninhabitable.
A farmer and an ex-NASA pilot, Joseph Cooper, is tasked to pilot a spacecraft for a mission to find a new
habitable planet for humans. A team of researchers have found a wormhole near Saturn and Cooper finds
himself between the pull of duty and the gravity of love.

The film explores themes of love, sacrifice, and the survival of the human species, while also delving into
complex scientific concepts such as time dilation, black holes, and the theory of relativity. With stunning visuals
and a thought-provoking narrative, Interstellar has become a landmark in modern science fiction cinema.

The film's composer, Hanz Zimmer, created a unique and memorable score that enhances the emotional depth
and epic scale of the story. He is also a good and a long time friend of Sir Christopher Nolan.

Fun Facts:
  • For the cornfield scene, Sir Christoper Nolan (who received his knighthood in December 2024) sought to grow 500 acres of corn,
    which he learned was feasible from his producing of Man Of Steel (2013). The corn was then sold, and actually made a profit.

  • The film's depiction of a black hole was so scientifically accurate that it led to scientific papers being published.

  • The black hole in the black hole scene was so scientifically accurate that it took approx 100 hours to render each frame in the
    physics and VFX engine. Meaning every second on screen took approx 100 days to render the final copy.
